Isochronism Check
What is an Isochronism Check?
Isochronism describes how consistently a watch keeps time as its mainspring winds down. A perfectly isochronous watch runs at the same rate whether fully wound or nearly run down.

Step 1  Full wind reading (3060 min after winding): After fully winding your watch, rest it for 3060 minutes to allow the mainspring torque to settle. Then record the amplitude, rate, and beat error from your Time Grapher.

Step 2  24-hour reading: Leave the watch stationary in a single position for 24 hours, then record the same three readings. The difference in rate between the two readings is the isochronal error.
